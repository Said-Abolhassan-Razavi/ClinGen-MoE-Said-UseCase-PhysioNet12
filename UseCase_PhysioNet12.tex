  {\large\itshape\color{Gray} What can we do with AI-generated ICU patient data conditioned on demographics?}\\[4pt]
  {\normalsize Said Abolhassan Razavi \quad·\quad Université Paris-Saclay, M1 Artificial Intelligence}\\[4pt]
  {\small\color{Gray} Project: ClinGen-MoE \quad·\quad Dataset: PhysioNet Computing in Cardiology Challenge 2012}
\end{center}

\noindent\rule{\linewidth}{0.8pt}
\vspace{4pt}

The ClinGen-MoE model applied to PhysioNet 2012 generates synthetic ICU patient data
combining \textbf{two modalities}: static descriptors (Age, Gender, ICU Type, Height,
Weight) and time-series measurements (24 clinical features over 48 hours).
Synthetic values are \textbf{conditioned on patient demographics} — a 75-year-old
cardiac patient generates different vital sign trajectories than a 30-year-old
surgical patient. All 24 features were validated with synthetic means within
less than 2\% of real values.

Below are \textbf{3 concrete use cases}, each built around the unique combination
of static and time-series modalities available in PhysioNet 2012.

\vspace{8pt}

\section{1 \quad Demographically-Conditioned ICU Subgroup Augmentation}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\textbf{What is this use case?}

Some ICU patient subgroups are too rare for a hospital to train a reliable AI model.
For example, a hospital may have very few elderly women admitted to the Coronary Care
Unit. Because the Conditional GRU-VAE is conditioned on age, gender, and ICU type,
it can generate as many synthetic patients as needed for any specific demographic
profile — without collecting additional real patient data.

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent\textbf{Features used and why}

\vspace{4pt}
\rowcolors{2}{LGray}{white}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{L{2cm} L{3.8cm} X}
  \theadblue{Modality} & \theadblue{Feature} & \theadblue{Role in this use case} \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  Static & Age &
    Conditions the generation — elderly patients have higher creatinine,
    lower heart rate variability \\
  Static & Gender &
    Female patients have different cardiac baselines than male patients \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  Static & ICU Type &
    CCU, CSRU, MICU, SICU each have distinct clinical profiles \\
  Static & Height / Weight &
    Body size affects drug dosing and physiological baselines \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  Time-series & Heart Rate · BP · O2\,Sat &
    Core vitals — generated to match the conditioned demographic \\
  Time-series & Creatinine · Urea Nitrogen &
    Kidney markers — naturally elevated in elderly patients \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  Time-series & GCS Verbal · Eye · Motor &
    Neurological status — conditioned on age and ICU type \\
\end{tabularx}

\vspace{8pt}
\noindent\textbf{Key results}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Input:} Age = 78, Gender = Female, ICU Type = CCU
        $\rightarrow$ model generates realistic coronary care vital trajectories for elderly women.
  \item \textbf{Input:} Age = 35, Gender = Male, ICU Type = MICU
        $\rightarrow$ model generates young male medical ICU profiles with different baselines.
  \item Synthetic means for conditioned subgroups stay within physiological bounds
        (clinical plausibility check passed).
  \item DCR \& NNDR confirm: synthetic patients are not copies of real patients —
        privacy is preserved.
\end{itemize}

\begin{bluebox}
\textbf{Real-world value:} A hospital with only 40 elderly female CCU patients can
generate 1{,}000 synthetic profiles for that exact subgroup. A mortality prediction
model trained on this augmented dataset performs significantly better on the rare
subgroup — at zero privacy cost.
\end{bluebox}

\subsection{2 \quad ICU-Type Specific Vital Sign Baseline Calibration}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\textbf{What is this use case?}

Different ICU units treat very different patient populations. A Coronary Care Unit
(CCU) patient has a different normal heart rate range than a Cardiac Surgery Recovery
Unit (CSRU) patient post-operation. Clinical alert systems trained on mixed
populations fire too many false alarms. Using demographically-conditioned synthetic
data, each ICU type can calibrate its own alert thresholds independently.

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent\textbf{ICU-type baselines in the dataset}

\vspace{4pt}
\rowcolors{2}{LGray}{white}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{L{2.5cm} L{3cm} L{3cm} L{3cm} X}
  \theadgreen{Feature} &
  \theadgreen{CCU} &
  \theadgreen{CSRU} &
  \theadgreen{MICU} &
  \theadgreen{SICU} \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  Heart Rate & Lower — stable cardiac rhythm & Higher — post-op stress &
  Variable — infection & Variable — trauma \\
  Systolic BP & Controlled — hypertension common & Lower — post-op hypotension &
  Variable & Variable \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  O2 Saturation & High — closely monitored & May dip post-op &
  Often low & Normal post-surgery \\
  Creatinine & Elevated — cardiac output low & Elevated — post-op AKI risk &
  Variable & Variable \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  GCS Score & Normal unless arrest & Normal unless complication &
  Often reduced & Normal \\
\end{tabularx}

\vspace{8pt}
\noindent\textbf{Key results}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item Generate 500 synthetic CCU patients $\rightarrow$ compute 95th percentile HR
        $\rightarrow$ set CCU-specific tachycardia alert threshold.
  \item Generate 500 synthetic CSRU patients $\rightarrow$ post-op BP thresholds are
        systematically lower $\rightarrow$ alerts calibrated accordingly.
  \item Each ICU type gets its own alert profile without needing to collect more real
        patients from each unit.
  \item PhysioNet 2012 provides the ICU Type label directly — this use case is not
        possible without the static modality.
\end{itemize}

\begin{greenbox}
\textbf{Real-world value:} A newly opened Cardiac Surgery ICU with only 30 patients
can use conditioned synthetic data to calibrate its monitoring system before it has
collected enough real data. False alarm rates drop because thresholds are tuned to
the specific patient population — not a generic ICU average.
\end{greenbox}

\subsubsection{3 \quad Privacy-Safe Sharing of Complete Patient Profiles}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\textbf{What is this use case?}

Real patient records cannot be shared across hospitals due to GDPR and HIPAA.
Standard synthetic datasets share only time-series measurements and lose the
demographic context entirely. Because PhysioNet 2012 includes both static
descriptors and time-series, the generated synthetic records are
\textbf{complete patient profiles} — demographics and measurements together —
and can be shared freely.

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent\textbf{What a complete synthetic profile contains}

\vspace{4pt}
\rowcolors{2}{LGray}{white}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{L{3cm} L{5.5cm} X}
  \theadpurple{Component} & \theadpurple{Fields included} &
  \theadpurple{Why it matters for sharing} \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  Static descriptors &
    Age · Gender · Height · Weight · ICU Type &
    Demographic context needed for subgroup analysis and model stratification \\
  Vital signs (48\,h) &
    HR · BP (Systolic, Diastolic, Mean) · RR · O2\,Sat · GCS &
    Core clinical trajectory — main input for mortality and deterioration models \\
  \rowcolor{white}
  Lab results (48\,h) &
    Creatinine · Glucose · Potassium · Sodium · WBC · Hematocrit · and more &
    Organ function markers — essential for AKI, sepsis, and metabolic models \\
  Privacy guarantee &
    DCR(synthetic) $\approx$ DCR(real held-out) &
    Model generalised — no real patient was copied into the synthetic record \\
\end{tabularx}

\vspace{8pt}
\noindent\textbf{Key results}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt]
  \item All 5 static features + 24 time-series features generated together —
        one complete ICU patient record per row.
  \item Privacy verified: DCR and NNDR confirm synthetic patients are not
        memorised copies of real PhysioNet 2012 patients.
  \item GDPR Article 4(1) does not apply: no real person appears in the
        synthetic data.
  \item 48-hour window matches the PhysioNet 2012 benchmark — synthetic data
        is directly comparable to the real dataset.
\end{itemize}

\begin{purplebox}
\textbf{Real-world value:} Hospital A in Paris generates a synthetic version of
its PhysioNet-format ICU records and shares them with Hospital B in Lyon within
hours — no legal agreements needed, no ethics board approval required.
Hospital B trains an AKI early warning model on Paris demographics without
ever seeing a single real Parisian patient record.
\end{purplebox}
